I use two environments, macOS and Windows. This post will details my macOS environment mainly as that's what I use most. I will do a follow-up post regarding my Windows based system at some point; it's now mainly a test system and gaming machine these days.

## Hardware

### MacBook 12”
The 12” MacBook (2015) initially was intended to be my main dev system; I found that I needed more screen real estate when working at my desk. So now the MacBook is what I use when I go to meetings or want to work when I am out and about.

It’s small and light which means its perfect for what I use it for now. Yeah, I could use an iPad, but I like to have the full macOS experience, and it allows me to do far more on a device which is just as portable as the iPad.

More info: [apple.com/uk/macbook](http://www.apple.com/uk/macbook/)

### Mac mini
I chose the Mac mini as it seemed like the ideal system for me to develop on while I am at my desk. It was small, cheap and had reasonable specs. I chose the 2.6GHz i5 2015 model and upgraded the 1TB 5400rpm HDD to a Samsung EVO 750 500GB SSD.

This little mac gets a lot of slack from people, but I have found it to be a workhorse and can run everything I throw at it. And since the upgrade to SSD, it's gotten even better!

I have this connected up to my two 27” Samsung displays.

More info: [apple.com/uk/mac-mini](http://www.apple.com/uk/mac-mini)

### Dual 27″ Samsung Curved Displays
Why not go for the Apple display I hear you say, well that's easy, cost! Both the Samsung displays were quite a reasonable price and half the cost of just one Apple display.

They are only 1080p, but this is a dev system, not a gaming system. So 1080p suits my needs perfectly.

I have been asked many times how do I connect two monitors to the Mac Mini. I investigated lots of options; some got quite costly. In the end, I settled for two [StarTech 1m DisplayPort to Mini DisplayPort](http://amzn.to/2eyVtRy) which work a treat. Occasionally when I boot up the Mac Mini they don’t detect it, but I’ve heard it's a common issue with 3rd party displays as Apple would rather you use their displays!

More info: [samsung.com/uk](http://www.samsung.com/uk/consumer/it/monitor/curved-monitor/LS27D590CS/EN)

### Apple wireless keyboard 2
I was using the wired Apple Keyboard, however recently moved to the Apple Wireless Keyboard 2. This awesome little keyboard is Bluetooth based which means I can use it on my iPad Pro too when I need to. Battery life is surprisingly good, and it's a pleasure to use. I still really like the wired keyboard due to the attached keypad and the two USB ports either side.

More info: [apple.com/uk/magic-accessories](http://www.apple.com/uk/magic-accessories)

### Apple Magic Mouse
Once again I could have gone for the Magic Mouse 2, but I was not sold on the recharging of it using a lightening cable. First off the charging port is in an odd place, so you can’t use it while it's charging. Secondly, I didn’t want to wait for it to charge fully - I’m too impatient!

I went for the Magic Mouse, which has the same design, minus the recharging. It uses two AA batteries, which are quick and easy to replace when it dies.

More info: [apple.com/uk/magic-accessories](http://www.apple.com/uk/magic-accessories)

### Apple Magic Trackpad 2
Now I did go for the Trackpad 2, for one simple reason - Force Touch. Force Touch was introduced in the 12” MacBook and since being introduced Apple has improved the uses for it in macOS.

I use the Trackpad 2 primarily when I am working in Photoshop or Sketch as it allows me to be a little more precise.

More info: [apple.com/uk/magic-accessories](http://www.apple.com/uk/magic-accessories)

## Software
Now on to software

### iTerm2
I work with Ruby on Rails, Jekyll and Git a lot. I use iTerm2 as it was recommended to me as a better replacement for the build in Terminal that comes with macOS.  
Link: [iterm2.com](https://www.iterm2.com)

### Chrome
To be more precise I use Chrome Canary. Chrome is my development browser of choice due to the Development Tools included - nothing else comes close!

Link: [google.co.uk/chrome](https://www.google.co.uk/chrome/browser/canary.html)

### Atom
I have used many editors over the years, and Atom is one of my favourites. Brought to you by the guys at GitHub it's become one of the most used apps on ALL of my systems.

It must be good because Microsoft has used the core code as the base for its Visual Studio Code app.

Link: [atom.io](https://atom.io)

### Sketch
Sketch has become my goto app for pretty much anything design related! It allows me to throw together flows, wireframes, designs and even icons in next to no time. I once used Photoshop, like most, but found that it didn't suit my needs entirely. It wasn't until and ex-colleague recommended Sketch to me that  I became and instant convert. Now it's one of my most used apps, and I recommend it to everyone!

Link: [sketchapp.com](https://www.sketchapp.com)

### iA Writer
I write a lot of markdown, from posts on this site to notes. So my markdown editor of choice is iA Writer, it is really nice to use and allows me to sync all my documents in iCloud. I have been beta testing a new app called [Bear](http://www.bear-writer.com/) recently. And this looks likes it has the potential to replace iA writer. 
Link: [ia.net/writer](https://ia.net/writer)

## Services
I also make use of a few services. I use most of them in my everyday work and are all incredibly cheap.

### Creative Cloud
I use Photoshop for a lot of my graphics work. It's been a regularly installed piece of software on my Mac and PC’s over the years.

I have tried others, such as Affinity Photo/Designer but always return to Photoshop.

Link: [adobe.com/creativecloud](http://www.adobe.com/creativecloud)

### Dropbox
Dropbox is handy as it allows me to share files with product owners. It also lets me back up some important files when needed.

Link: [dropbox.com](https://db.tt/1spLOUBz)

### Github
I use Github for all of my remote repositories. I have a personal account which recently added unlimited private repo's; this has allowed me to keep client work and test code as closed source.

Link: [github.com](https://www.github.com)

### Finch
A lot of the work I do is on my local machine, so it's not always easy to show the work I am doing to the product owners. And this is where Finch comes into play.

Finch is a simple app which allows me to provide a URL to the product owner so that they can view my localhost externally.

Link: [meetfinch.com](https://meetfinch.com)

There is a free alternative called [ngrok](https://ngrok.com). I did try ngrok but preferred Finch for its sheer ease and the ability to have a custom URL.

### Grammarly
Quite simply us developers are not known for our content writing abilities. I recently stumbled across Grammarly; it's a fantastic app which allows you check not only the spelling of content but the grammar.

Link: [grammarly.com](https://www.grammarly.com)
